â“ How To Use Gate Quote Pro

1 First: Set Up Your Company

Before creating quotes, go to Settings and enter your company information:

  • Company name, address, phone, and email
  • Your contractor license number
  • Your hourly labor rate (default is $125/hr)
  • Materials markup percentage (default is 30%)
  • Terms and conditions for your quotes
ğŸ’¡ Pro Tip: Your company info appears on every PDF quote, so make sure it's accurate!

2 Review Your Price List

The Price List page contains all materials and their costs. The app comes with common gate installation materials pre-loaded.

  • Review and update prices to match your actual costs
  • Add new materials as needed
  • Use the supplier price checker to look up current prices at Home Depot, Lowe's, Tractor Supply, or Walmart
  • Set markup percentages for each item (default 30%)
ğŸ’¡ Pro Tip: Keep your price list updated! Material costs change frequently.

3 Add Your Customers

Go to Customers to add your clients. You can also add customers directly when creating a quote.

  • Enter name, phone, email, and address
  • Add notes about the customer or property
  • View all quotes for each customer

4 Create a Quote

This is the main feature! Go to New Quote to create a professional quote:

  • Select or create a customer - Their info will appear on the quote
  • Enter gate specifications - Type, size, material, automation
  • Set site conditions - Ground type, slope, power distance
  • Click "Calculate" - This auto-estimates labor hours
  • Click "Auto-Suggest" - This adds materials based on your specs
  • Review and adjust - Add/remove items, change quantities
  • Save the quote - Then generate a PDF to send to your customer
ğŸ’¡ Pro Tip: The "Auto-Suggest" feature is smart! It knows that swing gates need hinges, sliding gates need track, automated gates need photoeyes, etc.

5 Manage Your Quotes

Go to Quote History to see all your quotes and track their status:

  • Draft - Quote is being worked on
  • Sent - Quote has been sent to customer
  • Accepted - Customer accepted the quote ğŸ‰
  • Declined - Customer declined

You can edit quotes, regenerate PDFs, or delete old quotes anytime.

6 Tips for Best Results

  • Always do a site visit before creating a final quote
  • Take photos and measurements
  • Account for any unusual site conditions in your notes
  • Review the auto-suggested materials - add or remove as needed
  • Adjust labor hours based on your experience with similar jobs
  • Include clear terms and a validity period on your quotes
